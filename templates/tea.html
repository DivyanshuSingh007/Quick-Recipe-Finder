Masala Tea Recipe

A flavorful spiced tea from India.

Ingredients

  • Water: 1 cup
  • Milk: 1 cup (you can adjust the ratio of milk and water to your preference)
  • Loose black tea leaves: 2 teaspoons (or 2 tea bags)
  • Sugar: 2 teaspoons (or to taste)
  • Spices (Masala):
  • Cinnamon stick: 1 small piece
  • reen cardamom pods: 2-3 (lightly crushed)
  • Cloves: 2-3
  • Fresh ginger: 1-inch piece, thinly sliced or grated
  • Black peppercorns: 2-3 (optional)
  • Fennel seeds: 1/2 teaspoon (optional)

Cooking Steps

  1. Boil the spices and water: In a saucepan, add the water, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, ginger, black peppercorns, and any optional spices like fennel seeds.
  2. Bring it to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for about 3-5 minutes. This step allows the spices to release their flavors.
  3. Add the black tea leaves to the simmering spice mixture. Continue to simmer for another 2-3 minutes for a strong tea flavor.
  4. Pour in the milk and add sugar (adjust the sweetness to your taste). Bring the mixture back to a boil while stirring occasionally. Be careful as the milk can overflow if left unattended.
  5. Once it starts boiling, lower the heat and let it simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing all the flavors to combine.
  6. Strain the tea into cups using a fine sieve or tea strainer to remove the spices and tea leaves.

Serve hot and enjoy your aromatic, spicy Masala Chai!
