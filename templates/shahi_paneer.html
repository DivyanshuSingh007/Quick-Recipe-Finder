Shahi Paneer Recipe

A rich and creamy Indian dish made with paneer in a luxurious gravy.

Ingredients

  • 200 grams paneer (cubed)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1-2 cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 cup tomato puree
  • 1/2 cup yogurt (whisked)
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts (soaked and ground into a paste)
  • 1/4 cup melon seeds or pumpkin seeds (soaked and ground into a paste)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon rose water or kewra water (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Cooking Steps

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee or oil and fry the paneer cubes until golden brown on all sides. Set them aside.
  2. In the same pan, add the remaining ghee or oil. Add the cumin seeds, cardamom pods, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, and cloves. Let them splutter for a few seconds.
  3. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown. Then, add ginger-garlic paste and cook until aromatic.
  4. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala (5-7 minutes).
  5. Add the whisked yogurt and cook for another 3-4 minutes.
  6. Add the cashew paste, melon seed paste, and cook for 5-7 minutes. Then, add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Stir well.
  7. Add the fried paneer cubes to the gravy. Let them cook in the gravy for about 5 minutes.
  8. Add cream, sugar, and saffron milk (optional). Stir everything together and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice.

Enjoy your delicious Shahi Paneer!
